Gun Gifts to Samaranch Exceed $2000

SALT LAKE CITY (AP) -- The retail value of the arsenal given by Salt Lake Olympics boosters to IOC president Juan Antonio Samaranch exceeds $2,000.

Samaranch said today he didn't want to insult the city by refusing the gifts.

``Of course, Utah is a state where guns are very popular,'' he said at a news conference in Zurich, Switzerland. ``I have visited Salt Lake City twice and I got a gun both times. I took it to Switzerland.''

So far, two rifles and a shotgun, all made by Browning Arms of Mountain Green, Utah, have been identified as going to Samaranch and there also may have been a handgun.

IOC officials say such gift-giving to the leader of the IOC is commonplace.

``The exchange of gifts on the occasion of visits by the IOC president is customary and it has almost become a matter of protocol to do so,'' said Francois Carrard, the IOC's director general.

Samaranch said he sees no problem in accepting the gifts, despite IOC rules that impose a $150 limit on gifts to committee members. He said the limit doesn't apply to him.

``The limit is for the members who participate in the elections,'' he said. ``Now, I do not take part in the voting and the elections.''

The Tribune de Geneve today quoted Samaranch as saying, ``I went to the American city twice before it was granted the 2002 Winter Games. On these occasions I did indeed receive a .22 Long Rifle and a Browning.

``I do remember very clearly the inscription on the case: `To President Juan Antonio Samaranch on behalf of the population of Salt Lake City.' These gifts were put down in a register, as is required by our internal rules. They are in my office in Lausanne and I am in a position to show them to any of my visitors,'' he said.

At the news conference, Samaranch said the two guns were in the Olympic Museum at IOC headquarters.

Carrard's comment was in response to a report Tuesday in The Salt Lake Tribune that Samaranch received two guns worth $1,000 from the Salt Lake bid committee a month before Salt Lake won the right to host the 2002 Winter Games.

The Tribune said in another copyright story today that another Browning rifle also went to Samaranch.

Carrard said Samaranch was given a rifle in a case with an engraved plaque that read: ``Presented to President Juan Antonio Samaranch from the Browning Arms Co. on behalf of the people of Salt Lake City.''
